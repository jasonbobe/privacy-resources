Privacy Resources for Open Science in Biomedical Research

The purpose of this resource is to aid researchers wishing to incorporate open science practice into their biomedical research study navigate privacy.

Jason Bobe

Sharing Lab, Icahn Institute at Mount Sinai School of Medicine

Here are some things I think are worth reviewing:

  • Educational Guide: Future Privacy Forum's new Visual Guide to Practical Data De-Identification
  • Classic Cases of What Not to Do: Danish researchers publicly released data from dating site, OK Cupid, without permission from users. An instant classic of how not behave. See summary of the case in the Daily Dot
  • Fun Facts: A recent study exploring Parkinson's disease gave participant two choices for sharing their data, either (a) share data only with the research team or (b) share data with ALL qualified researchers worldwide. Out of the first 10,000 enrolled participants, around 75% chose to share their data with ALL researchers worldwide. Wow. The ongoing statistics around sharing are published by Sage BioNetworks on their Bridge Server page, will be interesting to see if the trend toward broad sharing holds over time.
  • Debates: Generally, I am in the same camp as people when they say that they should "own their data" much like Obama did recently at one of the Precision Medicine Initiative roundtables. Technically though, ownership of data is the wrong mental model for expressing the desire for control over one's data, whether that means access, or effective voice. Be sure to read the piece by Barbara Evans on this topic.
  • Toolkits: John Wilbanks and friends have been innovating in a backwater of research governance that is informed consent. The consent form, as John likes to say is "written by a doctor, edited by a lawyer, and approved by a committee." The participant has gotten short shrift, until now. Check out his Participant-Centered Consent Toolkit and find some greats ways to instantly improve the process of informed consent in your study.
  • Nomenclature: Equal Access
